About

Hello! My name is Anne Oursler, and I'm a Computer Science student at Tufts University! I love learning, fencing, and hanging out with friends.

This website is both a portfolio of my work, and an oportunity for me to practice what I learned. That brings me to classes. At this time I have taken four courses from the Tufts Computer Science Department, and am taking two more this semester.

The spring of my freshman year I took Comp11, or Intro to Computer Science. It's taught in C++ and was a great time. Going into the class I thought there was no way I would ever want to seriously pursue computer science. By the end of the semester I had fallen in love, declared my major as compsci, and was excited to begin my journy into the world of computer science.

The fall of my sophmore year, three of my four courses were computer science related. I took Comp15, Datastructures and Algoritms, Comp20, Webprograming, and Comp61, Discrete Mathmatics. I learned so much and loved almost every minute of it! (Not gonna lie, being up at 4 am to finish comp15's final project, Boggle, was not my favorite moment.)

This upcoming semester, only two of my four courses will be computer science. I'm taking Comp40, or Machine Structure, and Comp50, or wearables. It should be a great semester, with lots of new projects. Stay tuned to see what I make next!

Please, explore my site, and be sure to check out my github aoursler!
