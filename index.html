About

Hello! My name is Anne Oursler, and I'm a Computer Science student at Tufts University! I love learning, fencing, coding, and hanging out with friends.

This website is both a portfolio of my work and an opportunity for me to practice what I've learned. That brings me to classes. I have taken six courses from the Tufts Computer Science Department and am taking two more this semester.

The spring of my freshman year I took Comp11 (Introduction to Computer Science). It's taught in C++ and I had a great time. Going into the class I thought there was no way I would want to pursue Computer Science. By the end of the semester, I had fallen in love, declared my major as compsci, and was excited to be on my joureny into the world of Computer Science.

The my sophmore year I took a total of five Computer Science related classes. I took Comp15 (Datastructures and Algorithms), Comp20 (Webprograming), and Comp61 (Discrete Mathmatics) in the Fall. In the spring I took Comp40 (Machinee Structure), and Comp50WD (Wearable Devices). I learned so much and loved almost every minute of it! (Not gonna lie, being up at 4 am to finish Comp15's final project, Boggle, was not my favorite moment.) These classes shaped who I am today. I learned about hardwares from the first day ("so whats a resistor?") to the final project. I learned that who I am is fundumentally happiest making, and I began 3D printing and designing far more out of class. I learned how to pair program and get seemingly imposible tasks done, from image compression to emulators and assembly.

This summer I wil be working for MITER, and attempting to learn everything I can in my spair time. Next Fall, I plan to take Comp105 (Programing Languages) and Comp116 (Security). I can't wait to learn all about functional programing as well as "Defence Against the Dark Arts" (Security). It should be a great semester with lots of new projects. Stay tuned to see what I make next!
