About

Hello! My name is Anne Oursler, and I'm a Computer Science student at Tufts University! I love learning, fencing, and hanging out with friends.

This website is both a portfolio of my work and an opportunity for me to practice what I've learned. That brings me to classes. I have taken four courses from the Tufts Computer Science Department and am taking two more this semester.

The spring of my freshman year I took Comp11 (Introduction to Computer Science). It's taught in C++ and I had a great time. Going into the class I thought there was no way I would want to pursue Computer Science. By the end of the semester, I had fallen in love, declared my major as compsci, and was excited to be on my joureny into the world of Computer Science.

The fall of my sophmore year, three of my courses were Computer Science related. I took Comp15 (Datastructures and Algorithms), Comp20 (Webprograming), and Comp61 (Discrete Mathmatics). I learned so much and loved almost every minute of it! (Not gonna lie, being up at 4 am to finish Comp15's final project, Boggle, was not my favorite moment.)

This semester, only two of my courses are Computer Science. I'm taking Comp40 (Machine Structure), and Comp50 (Wearable Devices). It should be a great semester with lots of new projects. Stay tuned to see what I make next!

Please explore my site, and be sure to check out my github, aoursler!
