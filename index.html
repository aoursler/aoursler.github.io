About

Hello! My name is Anne Oursler, and I'm a Computer Science student at Tufts University! I love learning, fencing, coding, and hanging out with friends.

This website is both a portfolio of my work and an opportunity for me to practice what I've learned. That brings me to classes. I have taken six courses from the Tufts Computer Science Department and am taking two more this semester.

The spring of my freshman year I took Comp11 (Introduction to Computer Science). It's taught in C++ and I had a great time. Going into the class I thought there was no way I would want to pursue Computer Science. By the end of the semester, I had fallen in love, declared my major as compsci, and was excited to be on my journeny into the world of Computer Science.

Then my sophmore year I took a total of five Computer Science related classes. I took Comp15 (Datastructures and Algorithms), Comp20 (Web Programming), and Comp61 (Discrete Mathmatics) in the fall. In the spring I took Comp40 (Machine Structure), and Comp50WD (Wearable Devices). I learned so much and loved almost every minute of it! (Not gonna lie, being up at 4 am to finish Comp15's final project, Boggle, was not my favorite moment.) Those classes shaped who I am today. I learned about hardware from the first day ("So what's a resistor?") to the final project. I learned that I am fundamentally happiest making, and I began 3D printing and designing far more out of class. I learned how to pair program and get seemingly imposible tasks done, from image compression to emulators and assembly.

This past summer I worked for MITRE, helping to create an IoT lab three as well as a IoT Unique Identification Challenge to further inspire contestants to find new methods of identifying devices on IoT networks. I am currently taking Comp105 (Programming Languages) and Comp116 (Security). It should be a great semester with lots of new projects, and hopefully a few on my own time. Stay tuned to see what I do next!
